## TLDR

- Ethereum‚Äôs core protocol work doubled down on scaling the L1, scaling L2s, and improving Ethereum UX in 2025. In addition to celebrating Ethereum‚Äôs 10 year anniversary with no downtime, two upgrades (Pectra and Fusaka) were shipped in 2025, helping to boost the gas limit from 30m to 60m, while target blobs were increased from 3 to 10. Alongside those scaling improvements, numerous UX improvements were implemented including smart account capabilities for externally owned accounts (EOAs) as well as stake consolidations for validators.
- All-time funding inflows reached $183m in 2025 (valued at time of donation), with $7.2m in new donations throughout the year. The top 5 donors for the year included Puffer, Starknet, Optimism RPGF, Enter DAO and LayerZero.
- Funding outflows, i.e. distributions of vested donations to Ethereum‚Äôs core protocol maintainers, totalled $12.4mm in 2025, an increase from the $10m distributed in 2024. Overall, the median amount distributed over the last 12 months totalled $62k per member.
- A new funding program was announced in 2025: ‚ÄúSponsor a Core Dev‚Äù. Intended to complement the 1% Pledge, it is designed to connect donors more directly with core devs, while unlocking a more flexible donation path for teams that are post-TGE.
- 2025 saw the arrival of a new brand identity for Protocol Guild, including a standalone website! On the content side the year was highlighted by our first-ever core developer compensation report, as well as numerous other blog posts, conference talks, podcasts and social media posts.
- Protocol Guild‚Äôs governance got an upgrade with the Agora DAO migration, enabling control of funding distribution contracts to be transferred from a multisig to the DAO.

## Core Protocol Development Updates

The Ethereum protocol celebrated its 10 year anniversary in 2025, and a big theme of that anniversary was ‚Äú10 years, no downtime‚Äù. This unrivaled track record of uptime is a testament to the incredible work being done by Ethereum‚Äôs core protocol contributors, considering the scale of the coordination required for these upgrades, as well as the amount of funds that are being secured by Ethereum. In addition to this incredible milestone, 2025 also saw two major upgrades shipped to mainnet.

### Pectra

In May 2025, the first upgrade of the year went live with [Pectra](https://forkcast.org/upgrade/pectra) - the biggest upgrade in Ethereum‚Äôs history - bringing many improvements to the core protocol (see the list at [EIP-7600](https://eips.ethereum.org/EIPS/eip-7600)). Key user experience enhancements included adding programmability to Ethereum‚Äôs default accounts (EOAs or externally-owned accounts) via EIP-7702. This upgrade gave all EOAs account abstraction powers, including batching and gas-free transactions. Pectra also introduced improvements to the staking UI, including stake consolidation and increased balance caps (EIP 7251), as well as faster deposits (EIP 6110). Finally, scaling-related enhancements on the L2 side included doubling the target number of blobs (EIP 7691).

![Pectra](/20260129-annual-report-2025/Pectra.png)
[https://dune.com/butta_ethereum/maxeb-0x02-and-consolidations](https://dune.com/butta_ethereum/maxeb-0x02-and-consolidations)

### Fusaka

While Pectra was in the final stages of being released,  core developers were already preparing to ship the second upgrade of the year, [Fusaka](https://forkcast.org/upgrade/fusaka), which landed in December (learn more at  [EIP-7607](https://eips.ethereum.org/EIPS/eip-7607)). It should be noted that two upgrades in one year of this scale and complexity are unprecedented for Ethereum‚Äôs core protocol development - an excellent reminder of the incredible amount of work that goes into these upgrades! The headliner of the Fusaka upgrade was PeerDAS, which optimized the data availability layer used by Ethereum‚Äôs L2s. PeerDAS essentially fulfilled Ethereum‚Äôs vision of ‚Äúsharding‚Äù, by allowing mainnet nodes to store a fraction of ‚Äùblob‚Äù data - the unit of account for L2 data availability - while still enabling that data to be fully reproducible across all nodes. This change enabled immediate increases in bandwidth, and set a foundation for  additional future increases. Fusaka also enhanced L1 scalability by increasing the block gas limit from 45m to 60m. Finally, user experience improvements such as native passkey and secp256r1 support (EIP-7951) rounded out the upgrade.

![Fusaka](/20260129-annual-report-2025/Fusaka.png)
[https://dune.com/queries/3757544/6319515](https://dune.com/queries/3757544/6319515)

### Glamsterdam

Parallel to the upgrades that were shipped in 2025, work was already underway on the next upgrade planned for 2026: [Glamsterdam](https://forkcast.org/upgrade/glamsterdam). Its headliners include enshrined proposer-builder separation aka ePBS (EIP-7732), which will enable significant L1 scalability increases by optimizing the block production pipeline, as well as block level access lists aka BALs (EIP-7928) which also supports significant L1 scalability unlocks by introducing parallelization to execution clients.

Here‚Äôs to another 10 years with no downtime!

_It‚Äôs important to note that not all core protocol maintainers are members of Protocol Guild; Ethereum stewardship comes from a broader community. Still, Protocol Guild  represents a meaningful snapshot of the cohort doing critical, long-horizon protocol work._

## Membership Curation

Protocol Guild is defined by its membership registry of Ethereum‚Äôs core protocol contributors. Since being launched in 2022, Protocol Guild has audited this membership registry on a quarterly basis to ensure the list only includes active contributors. As part of this membership curation, Protocol Guild also saw iterative refinement to its eligibility framework and [documentation](https://protocol-guild.readthedocs.io/) throughout the year.

## Smart Contract Architecture

Protocol Guild‚Äôs onchain smart contract architecture received significant overhauls in 2026, with the adoption of Splitv2.1 contracts to help streamline onchain funding flows, the expansion of funding contracts onto new L2s, as well as the deployment of a new [Agora DAO](https://www.agora.xyz/) for the membership to control the Splitv2.1 contracts.

![Architecture](/20260129-annual-report-2025/Architecture.png)

To quickly recap, Protocol Guild‚Äôs donation address is an [immutable vesting contract](https://app.splits.org/accounts/0x25941dc771bb64514fc8abbce970307fb9d477e9/) which vests tokens linearily over 4 years. Vested funds get pushed into a [Pass-Through Wallet](https://app.splits.org/accounts/0x25941dc771bb64514fc8abbce970307fb9d477e9/) (PTW), before being pushed to a [Split contract](https://app.splits.org/accounts/0xd982477216dadd4c258094b071b49d17b6271d66/?chainId=1) for distribution to Protocol Guild Members. The Split contract is mutable, as it is updated every quarter with Protocol Guild‚Äôs up-to-date membership. The PTW is also mutable, to enable us to swap out Split contracts when needed (since the vesting contracts recipient cannot be changed.) All these contracts are all built and maintained by the incredibly talented [Splits team](https://splits.org/).

In 2025 the Splits team released an upgraded version of their Split contract, [Splitv2.1](https://docs.splits.org/core/split-v2), which Protocol Guild adopted on Ethereum mainnet, as well as [Arbitrum](https://app.splits.org/accounts/0xd982477216dadd4c258094b071b49d17b6271d66/?chainId=42161), [Base](https://app.splits.org/accounts/0xd982477216dadd4c258094b071b49d17b6271d66/?chainId=8453) and [Optimism](https://app.splits.org/accounts/0xd982477216dadd4c258094b071b49d17b6271d66/?chainId=10). Alongside this, new vesting contracts were also deployed on [Arbitrum](https://app.splits.org/accounts/0x7F8DCFd764bA8e9B3BA577dC641D5c664B74c47b/?chainId=42161), [Base](https://app.splits.org/accounts/0xd16713A5D4Eb7E3aAc9D2228eB72f6f7328FADBD/?chainId=8453) and [Optimism](https://app.splits.org/accounts/0x58ae0925077527a87D3B785aDecA018F9977Ec34/?chainId=10) for the first time.

In the past, these Split contracts were controlled by Protocol Guild‚Äôs [6/10 multisigs](https://protocol-guild.readthedocs.io/en/latest/02-onchain-architecture.html#multisigs). However, with the launch of [Protocol Guild‚Äôs new Agora DAO,](http://gov.protocolguild.org/) this control has been migrated directly to the DAO. Huge thanks to the [Agora team](https://www.agora.xyz/) for enabling a smooth transition from our [Moloch V3 DAO](https://admin.daohaus.club/#/molochv3/0x1/0x412a32dd71357bd12337f4408168df903f90cbd3/proposals). Reducing multisig dependencies was a key goal for 2025, and we are pleased to have made big strides in that regard.

## 2026 Plans

Fundraising conditions remained challenging and several major closes slipped into 2026, but the core strategy is clearer than ever: broaden the number of ‚Äúways to say yes‚Äù to funding Ethereum‚Äôs essential dependencies, and continue upgrading our operational credibility so the ecosystem can trust it at scale.

One point that must be communicated, is that the largest source underwriting Ethereum‚Äôs value is the continuation of core development and that, simultaneously, this process remains currently underfunded by a significant margin. Core contributors are earning 50-60% below cash compensation offers outside of core development, with an even wider margin in risk compensation. While the popular refrain from the ecosystem alludes to a reliance on entities like the Ethereum Foundation for perpetual funding, [looming budget constraints](https://blog.ethereum.org/2025/06/04/ef-treasury-policy?) prove this is not a sustainable strategy.

Since Protocol Guild‚Äôs 2022 inception, it has processed inflows worth hundreds of millions of dollars and distributed over $36 million to the individuals delivering Ethereum core development outcomes. Through a combination of value sources (yield, tokens, revenue, excess funds), the ecosystem **must** come together to recognize this critical constraint and proactively seek solutions to create a sustainable funding paradigm. Protocol Guild is a tried-and-true mechanism for routing that funding to the ecosystem‚Äôs most critical actors. With focus and buy-in, 2026 can become a crucial turning point in establishing enduring funding streams for this work.

With this in mind, our priorities going into 2026 are;
